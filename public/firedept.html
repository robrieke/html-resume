Raised by the Fire Department

I am proud of my family's legacy of community service, especially in their contributions to the creation and professional reputation of the Animas Fire Department. I rode along with my father to car accidents, fires, and medical incidents from a young age and always wanted to make the same difference that he and the rest of the firefighters made in everybody's lives.

While I was often too young to help, I tried to participate in as many fire department activities as I could. I attended trainings, helped wash and maintain equipment, and read as much emergency service literature as I could. I spent every moment I could at the Animas Fire stations, including time that would have been better dedicated to my high school education (oops...). The only thing stopping me from becoming an official volunteer was their minimum age restriction of 18.

When I turned 16, I joined the nearby Hermosa Cliff Fire Department as a volunteer firefighter. Under their umbrella, I was able to report to incidents in both Hermosa Cliff and Animas Fire's territory. I became an unstoppable force of community service, and responded to probably more than a thousand medical incidents, vehicle accidents, and fires. For several years I was lucky enough to carry a camera and take pictures of firefighters in action.

While in high school, I took several college courses and received my Firefighter I, Firefighter II, and Emergency Medical Technician - Basic certifications. I was able to attend four annual Colorado Firefighter Academy sessions and one New Mexico International Association of Arson Investigators conference.

I was as active as I could be on both fire departments until I had to take a military leave of absense to join the U.S. Navy. Shortly after I left, they merged with the Durango Fire Department and Mercy Ambulance to form the Durango Fire and Rescue Authority. When I left the Navy and came back home, everything was different. In a good way. The community was getting even better service than before.

Why am I writing all this in a resume? Because the desire to better myself to help those around me is an integral part of me, and it is important for everyone to know that.
